Business Process Model and Notation (BPMN)

If you are a student in Management Software and Technology, you are probably interested in knowing more about these strange business process drawings. These drawings follow a standard called Business Process Model and Notation (BPMN), which is a way of communicating business procedures in a graphical notation. It is highly valuable because it is difficult to communicate what exactly organizations do throughout their processes.

On this page, I will discuss a few of the most common symbols. These images are borrowed from the OMG group cited below. You are encouraged to copy the images provided in your weekly exercises.

References

Object Management Group Standards Development Organization (2023). BPMN Quick Guide. https://www.bpmnquickguide.com/view-bpmn-quick-guide/

Common BPMN Symbols


Events

Events represents something that happens, such as a process start or process end.

Activities

Activities are units of work that an individual or organization performs.

Gateways

Gateways control the flow of a process and guide the conditions for a decision.

Flow

Flow represents the choices and actions that lead from one step to the next.
